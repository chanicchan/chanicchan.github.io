Section A

There are 33 questions. Questions marked with * involve knowledge of the extension component.

1.
A 200 g block of a hot metal is dropped into a copper calorimeter of mass 100 g containing 300 g of water. The temperature of the water and calorimeter rises from 20$^\circ$C to 30$^\circ$C. What was the initial temperature of the metal block?
(Given: Specific heat capacity of water = 4200 J kg$^{-1}$ $^\circ$C$^{-1}$; copper = 390 J kg$^{-1}$ $^\circ$C$^{-1}$; metal = 450 J kg$^{-1}$ $^\circ$C$^{-1}$)
  1. 129.9$^\circ$C
  2. 144.3$^\circ$C
  3. 172.1$^\circ$C
  4. 183.3$^\circ$C
2.
A block of dry ice (solid carbon dioxide) at -78.5$^\circ$C turns directly into gas at the same temperature. Which statement correctly describes the change in the carbon dioxide molecules?
  1. Their average kinetic energy increases.
  2. Their average potential energy decreases.
  3. Their average kinetic energy is unchanged but their average potential energy increases.
  4. Both their average kinetic and potential energy increase.
3.
An ideal gas is held in a cylinder by a frictionless piston. The initial volume of the gas is $1.5 \times 10^{-3}$ m$^3$ at a pressure of $1.0 \times 10^5$ Pa and temperature 300 K. The gas is heated to 400 K and its volume expands to $2.5 \times 10^{-3}$ m$^3$. What is the new pressure of the gas?
  1. $0.80 \times 10^5$ Pa
  2. $0.95 \times 10^5$ Pa
  3. $1.25 \times 10^5$ Pa
  4. $2.22 \times 10^5$ Pa
4.
A robot moves from start point P to end point Q in 20 s by travelling 8 m East and then 6 m North. What is the difference between its average speed and the magnitude of its average velocity?
  1. 0 m s$^{-1}$
  2. 0.2 m s$^{-1}$
  3. 0.5 m s$^{-1}$
  4. 0.7 m s$^{-1}$
5.
A uniform horizontal rod of length 2.0 m and weight 50 N is pivoted to a wall. It supports a 200 N sign and is held in place by a cable as shown. What is the tension T in the cable?
  1. 300.5 N
  2. 450.0 N
  3. 540.8 N
  4. 811.2 N
6.
Two masses, $m_1 = 4$ kg and $m_2 = 6$ kg, are connected by a light inextensible string over a frictionless pulley (an Atwood machine). When the system is released from rest, what is the acceleration of the masses? (Take $g = 9.81$ m s$^{-2}$)
  1. 1.96 m s$^{-2}$
  2. 3.92 m s$^{-2}$
  3. 4.91 m s$^{-2}$
  4. 9.81 m s$^{-2}$
7.
A 60 kg person stands at rest on a 20 kg skateboard on a frictionless horizontal surface. The person then throws a 5 kg bag horizontally with a velocity of 8 m s$^{-1}$ relative to the ground. What is the final recoil velocity of the person and the skateboard?
  1. -0.5 m s$^{-1}$
  2. -0.67 m s$^{-1}$
  3. -0.8 m s$^{-1}$
  4. -1.0 m s$^{-1}$
8.
A trolley of mass 2.0 kg is moving at 1.0 m s$^{-1}$. A time-varying force F acts on it in the direction of motion. The force-time graph is shown below. What is the final speed of the trolley at t=6 s?
  1. 8.0 m s$^{-1}$
  2. 9.0 m s$^{-1}$
  3. 12.0 m s$^{-1}$
  4. 16.0 m s$^{-1}$
